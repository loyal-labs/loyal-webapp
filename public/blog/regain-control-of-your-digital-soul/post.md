Every transfer you make on a public chain is a permanent, searchable record. Every balance is readable by anyone with a block explorer. That's the default, and most people never chose it.

## Privacy is the default, not the upgrade

Loyal flips the assumption. Your dollars stay shielded from the public chain, your transfers between Loyal users are arithmetic on confidential deposit accounts rather than on-chain movements, and your shielded USDC keeps earning yield while it stays private.

### What "control" actually means

- **Self-custody.** Keys live in your passkey, extension, or app — never with us.
- **Selective disclosure.** Hierarchical viewing keys let you grant a read-only view to an accountant or auditor without giving up your private key.
- **No identity at the wallet layer.** No email, no KYC to create a wallet.

> You shouldn't have to choose between staying private and staying compliant. Viewing keys make disclosure a decision you make, not a default you inherit.

The point isn't secrecy for its own sake. It's that *you* decide what's visible, to whom, and when.
